# TARA Staff Manual

## Template Automation & Revenue Agent

TARA helps Camelot turn routine property management paperwork into organized, trackable, billable work.

The purpose is simple:

- make documents faster
- stop losing track of small billable services
- create clean invoice drafts
- archive every generated document
- reward staff for finding and completing legitimate revenue tasks

TARA does not replace judgment. It helps the team identify work that may be billable, prepare it correctly, and send it to a manager for approval.

## The Big Idea

Many property management tasks take real time but are often forgotten, waived by accident, or buried in emails.

Examples:

- alteration agreements
- decorative agreements
- move-in / move-out packages
- refinance questionnaires
- lender questionnaires
- purchase packages
- sublet packages
- lease renewals
- RPIE prep
- MDR / HPD registration support
- tax abatement forms
- violation response packets
- vendor W-9 and COI onboarding
- transition request letters

TARA helps Camelot convert this work into a clean workflow:

Request received -> template selected -> document generated -> billable task queued -> manager approves -> invoice drafted -> payment tracked -> file archived.

## Staff Revenue Incentive

Camelot may pay staff up to 30% of approved and collected template/service fees generated through TARA.

This is an incentive for identifying, preparing, and completing real billable work.

Important rules:

- The fee must be approved by a manager.
- The invoice must be actually collected.
- The work must be properly documented.
- The fee must be allowed by the building agreement, fee schedule, governing documents, or client approval.
- No one earns a revenue share for waived, rejected, unpaid, duplicate, or unauthorized charges.

Recommended incentive structure:

| Role | Suggested Share |
| --- | ---: |
| Staff member who identifies the billable task | 5% to 10% |
| Staff member who prepares the package/document | 10% to 15% |
| Staff member who completes follow-through and gets it invoice-ready | 5% to 10% |
| Maximum total staff incentive | 30% |

If more than one person works on the task, the manager can split the incentive.

## What Staff Should Look For

When a resident, owner, board member, buyer, seller, broker, vendor, or landlord asks for something, ask:

1. Is this a normal included management task?
2. Is this a separate administrative, compliance, legal, accounting, financing, or document service?
3. Is there a building fee schedule or Schedule A charge for this?
4. Who requested it?
5. Who should pay for it?
6. Did we create a document, packet, certificate, form, report, or special response?
7. Should this be added to the Billable Task Queue?

If the answer is unclear, queue it for manager review. Do not guess.

## Common Billable Examples

### Alteration Agreement

Usually paid by the unit owner or shareholder.

Use when an owner wants to renovate, alter, repair, or improve their unit.

### Decorative Agreement

Usually paid by the unit owner or shareholder.

Use for smaller unit-level work that still requires building approval, insurance, or documentation.

### Move-In / Move-Out Package

Usually paid by the resident, renter, buyer, seller, or unit owner depending on the building rules.

Use when someone moves into or out of the building and needs forms, rules, deposits, elevator coordination, insurance, or staff scheduling.

### Refinance / Lender Questionnaire

Usually paid by the unit owner or shareholder.

Use when a bank, lender, broker, or owner requests building financial, insurance, questionnaire, recognition agreement, or closing-related information.

### Purchase / Sale / Board Package

Usually paid by the buyer, seller, broker, or unit owner depending on the building fee schedule.

Use when a unit is being sold, transferred, reviewed by the board, or prepared for closing.

### RPIE / MDR / Tax Abatement

Usually paid by the association, building owner, or landlord.

Use when the work goes beyond normal management and requires special filings, research, document gathering, or reporting.

### Violation Response Packet

Usually paid by the association or owner, unless a unit owner, contractor, or vendor caused the issue and chargeback is allowed.

Use when Camelot has to gather records, coordinate a correction, prepare a response, or organize supporting documents.

## How To Use TARA

### Step 1: Open TARA

Go to the TARA section in Camelot OS.

Start from the Dashboard. Review:

- pending billable tasks
- invoice drafts
- generated documents
- recent revenue opportunities

### Step 2: Choose A Template

Open the Template Library.

Pick the correct template category:

- Property Management
- Co-op / Condo
- Rental / Leasing
- Compliance
- Accounting
- Vendor
- Resident Services
- Brokerage

Then select the template you need.

### Step 3: Enter The Request Details

Fill in:

- property name
- property address
- requester name
- requester role
- requester email
- payer role
- due date
- manager assigned
- notes
- source documents or attachments

Use the notes field to explain why the work was requested.

### Step 4: Preview The Document

Review the generated document before sending or billing.

Check:

- property name
- address
- resident or owner name
- fee language
- dates
- spelling
- building-specific rules
- Camelot branding

Do not send a document that has bad facts.

### Step 5: Add To Billable Task Queue

If the task may be billable, click Add to Billable Task Queue.

TARA will recommend:

- fee code
- payer role
- amount
- approval status
- review reason

### Step 6: Manager Review

A manager or account manager reviews the task.

The manager chooses:

- approve
- waive
- reject
- request more information
- convert to invoice draft

No invoice should go out without approval.

### Step 7: Create Invoice Draft

When approved, TARA creates an invoice draft.

The invoice should include:

- invoice number
- property
- payer
- task description
- fee
- due date
- related document
- manager approval record

### Step 8: Send, Track, Archive

After the invoice is sent and paid, the task is marked collected.

The generated document, invoice, payment status, and staff incentive record should be archived.

## What Not To Do

Do not:

- tell residents a task is free when the fee schedule says otherwise
- bill a resident, owner, buyer, seller, or renter without approval
- guess who should pay
- create duplicate invoices
- skip manager review
- promise legal, accounting, tax, insurance, or financing advice
- use TARA to hide unclear fees

When in doubt, queue the task and ask for review.

## Manager Checklist

Before approving a billable task, confirm:

- the task was actually requested
- the payer is correct
- the fee is allowed
- the amount is reasonable
- the document was reviewed
- the work is not already included in the management fee
- the invoice description is clear
- the staff incentive is appropriate

## Revenue Example

A shareholder requests a refinance questionnaire.

TARA identifies:

- task type: refinance questionnaire
- payer: shareholder
- fee: $350
- approval required: yes

The account manager approves the task. The invoice is sent and paid.

If Camelot uses a 30% incentive pool, up to $105 may be shared with staff who identified, prepared, and completed the task.

Camelot keeps the balance while also creating a cleaner record, faster service, and better client accountability.

## Why This Matters

TARA is not just a document tool. It is a revenue discipline tool.

It helps Camelot:

- create faster resident service
- reduce missed billing
- improve manager oversight
- standardize documents
- reward staff for useful work
- build a clean audit trail
- make small administrative services profitable

The goal is not to nickel-and-dime clients. The goal is to charge properly for extra work, communicate clearly, and stop giving away specialized administrative time by accident.
