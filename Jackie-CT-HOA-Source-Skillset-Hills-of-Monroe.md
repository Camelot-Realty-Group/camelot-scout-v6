## 1. Core Objective

Jackie’s Connecticut HOA objective is different from the NYC co-op/condo workflow. For Monroe, CT and similar HOA communities, Jackie should not force NYC-only sources such as HPD, DOB BIS, DOB NOW, DOF, ACRIS, ECB/OATH, DHCR, or LL97 into the report as if they apply.

Instead, Jackie should operate as a Connecticut HOA executive-management research engine. The goal is to help Camelot identify:

- Property identity, parcels, ownership, declarations, common-area land records, association entity records, and liens.
- HOA governance signals: board, officers, manager, bylaws, declaration, amendments, annual reports, minutes, budgets, reserves, insurance, and vendor files.
- Municipal risk signals: permits, zoning, wetlands, fire marshal, health department, planning/zoning, stormwater, building, and open complaints where available.
- Insurance and restoration context: public adjusters, carrier/claim status, scope, estimate, contractor, change orders, payment status, photo documentation, and resident communication plan.
- Field operations needs: snow/ice, drainage, roofing, roads, lighting, landscaping, emergency response, deferred maintenance, reserve funding, and vendor accountability.
- Board-facing value story: operational stabilization, financial transparency, claims oversight, regional field operations, technology reporting, and long-term asset preservation.

Jackie’s tone for CT HOA work should be calm, executive, and operationally intelligent: facts strict, story tailored.

## 2. Connecticut Source Map For Jackie

### A. Monroe Town Clerk / Land Records

Use for deeds, declarations, easements, amendments, liens, maps, mortgages, releases, association-related recorded documents, and property fraud alerts.

Source: https://www.monroect.gov/p/land-records  
Monroe notes that land records and maps are used to research and create a system of information about property in the town. Monroe also points users to online land records. Older information before 1823 may be in Stratford or Shelton.

Skill rule:
- Search Monroe land records first when looking for declaration, bylaws, amendments, common-area parcels, easements, lien releases, master deeds, and HOA-related documents.
- If online records are incomplete, mark `manual_required: Monroe Town Clerk`.

### B. Monroe Assessor / Vision Government Solutions / GIS

Use for parcel identity, assessed value, ownership, property cards, map/lot, building characteristics, land area, GIS mapping, and town revaluation context.

Source: https://www.monroect.gov/p/assessor  
Monroe states the Assessor provides property valuation, exemption information, mapping, and ownership records. Monroe’s 2024 revaluation data is available through Vision Government Solutions for Monroe.

Skill rule:
- Use assessor/Vision data as the CT equivalent of NYC DOF/PLUTO for property facts.
- Use GIS parcel boundaries and property card details to avoid wrong-property identity.
- For HOA communities, identify both individual unit parcels and common-area parcels when available.

### C. Monroe Online Permitting / OpenGov

Use for building permits, health, inland wetlands, fire marshal, planning and zoning, zoning complaints, and local municipal permit workflow.

Source: https://www.monroect.gov/p/online-permitting  
Monroe states its online permitting system includes Building, Health, Inland Wetlands, Fire Marshal, and Planning & Zoning departments.

Skill rule:
- Use Monroe OpenGov as the CT equivalent of DOB/DOB NOW/permits.
- Search for active building permits, zoning complaints, wetlands permits, fire marshal records, and restoration/construction permit signals.
- If the system is not searchable by address or association name, mark `manual_required: Monroe OpenGov / department follow-up`.

### D. Monroe Inland Wetlands / Environmental Review

Use for drainage, watercourse, wetland, grading, paving, excavation, stormwater, and regulated-activity risk.

Source: https://www.monroect.gov/p/inland-wetlands  
Monroe’s Inland Wetlands Commission enforces the Inland Wetlands and Watercourses Act and handles permit review, enforcement/permitting inspections, compliance assessments, and legal coordination with the Town Attorney.

Skill rule:
- For HOA communities with ice, drainage, roofing, road, or stormwater issues, Jackie must check whether wetlands/watercourse or land-use approvals may be relevant.
- Add this to restoration/project oversight workflow.

### E. Monroe Tax Collector / Local Property Tax

Use for local tax bills, delinquencies, municipal liens, payment status, mill-rate context, and municipal tax risk.

Source: https://www.monroect.gov/tax-collector  
CT property tax questions are directed to the town tax collector for the property location. State source: https://portal.ct.gov/revenue/property-tax/

Skill rule:
- Treat the local tax collector as the CT equivalent of NYC DOF property tax lookup.
- For HOAs, check common-area parcels and association-owned parcels separately.

### F. CT Secretary of the State / Business Search / UCC

Use for HOA legal entity status, registered agent, annual reports, principals/officers where available, entity address, dissolved/active status, and UCC filings.

Source: https://portal.ct.gov/sots/business-services/business-services  
Source: https://business.ct.gov/business-services  
The CT Secretary of State states that all Business and UCC filings are handled at business.ct.gov, including business searches and UCC liens.

Skill rule:
- Search the association name, likely variants, management company, restoration contractor, public adjuster entity, and major vendors.
- Record entity status, agent, annual report status, and UCC signals.
- Use UCC search for lien-style risk against association/vendor entities, not as a substitute for town land records.

### G. CT Judicial Branch / Case Lookup

Use for civil litigation, foreclosure, housing, small claims, contract disputes, association disputes, vendor litigation, insurance-related disputes, and collection matters.

Source: https://www.jud.ct.gov/  
Source: https://vvv.jud.ct.gov/external/media/faq.htm  
The CT Judicial Branch notes online access is available through Case Look-up for civil, criminal/motor vehicle, family, housing, and small claims cases.

Skill rule:
- Search by HOA legal name, board entity, management company, public adjuster, restoration contractor, and known vendors.
- Flag active cases, foreclosure, lien, collection, contractor, and insurance disputes.
- If case search is blocked or ambiguous, mark manual review.

### H. CT Department of Consumer Protection / Community Association Managers

Use for Community Association Manager credentialing, complaints against third-party CAMs, and manager licensing context.

Source: https://portal.ct.gov/dcp/condominiums  
DCP states it credentials Community Association Managers hired by HOAs, condo associations, and cooperatives. DCP also notes it does not hold hearings on condo/HOA regulation disputes except manager licensing complaints; such HOA/condo regulatory disputes are addressed in court.

Skill rule:
- For CT HOA proposals, search whether current/previous management has CAM credentials and complaint signals.
- Do not imply DCP adjudicates HOA governance disputes; use court records for governance/litigation claims.

### I. Connecticut Common Interest Ownership Act / Association Records

Use for board governance, records request checklist, financial transparency, minutes, budgets, reserves, executive board records, bylaws, rules, annual report, and owner list requirements.

Source: https://www.cga.ct.gov/2022/sup/chap_828.htm  
Source: https://cga.ct.gov/2023/pub/chap_828.htm  
Chapter 828 Sec. 47-260 requires associations to retain detailed receipts/expenditures, accounting records including reserve accounts, minutes, unit owner records, organizational documents, bylaws, rules, financial statements and tax returns, board/officer list, and annual reports where applicable.

Skill rule:
- Jackie’s HOA onboarding checklist should ask for the documents described by Sec. 47-260.
- Use this as the legal basis for why Camelot needs organized records before finalizing scope and transition.

### J. CT Insurance Department / Public Adjusters / Claims

Use for public adjuster licensing, claim handling expectations, disaster/claims guidance, adjuster contract rules, insurance complaint routing, and restoration oversight context.

Source: https://portal.ct.gov/cid/licensing/license-types/public-adjuster  
CT Insurance Department defines a public adjuster as a person or business that adjusts loss or damage claims on behalf of an insured under policies covering fire or other hazards. Public adjuster licenses expire every two years.  
Source: https://portal.ct.gov/cid/-/media/CID/1_Brochures/Claims-Disaster-Guide.pdf

Skill rule:
- For any winter/ice/flood/restoration HOA matter, Jackie must ask for claim number, carrier, policy, adjuster, public adjuster, restoration contractor, scope, estimate, photos, invoices, payments, supplements, and board communications.
- Verify public adjuster licensing/status if named.

### K. Market / Regional Context

Use for HOA market narrative, local conditions, property values, reserve pressure, insurance inflation, storm exposure, contractor availability, and comparable HOA/community management needs.

Potential sources:
- Monroe municipal site and public meeting records: https://www.monroect.gov/
- Assessor/Vision property records: https://www.monroect.gov/p/assessor
- Town planning/zoning and wetlands pages: https://www.monroect.gov/p/inland-wetlands
- CT judicial/court lookup: https://www.jud.ct.gov/
- CT DCP CAM licensing/complaint context: https://portal.ct.gov/dcp/condominiums
- CT Insurance Department: https://portal.ct.gov/cid/
- CT Secretary of State / business.ct.gov: https://business.ct.gov/business-services
- Land records: https://www.monroect.gov/p/land-records

Skill rule:
- Use official public sources first.
- Use private aggregators only as secondary cross-checks and never as sole factual authority.

## 4. Hills of Monroe HOA Search Workflow

When user enters `Hills of Monroe HOA, Monroe, CT` or selects `HOA Recovery`, Jackie should run or prompt this workflow:

### Step 1: Identity
- Confirm association legal name and variants.
- Search CT Secretary of State / business.ct.gov.
- Search Monroe land records for declarations, amendments, maps, common-area parcels, and association instruments.
- Search Monroe Assessor/Vision for parcels and property cards.

### Step 2: Municipal Risk
- Search Monroe OpenGov permitting.
- Search Building, Fire Marshal, Planning & Zoning, Inland Wetlands, and Health department categories.
- Search tax collector/common-area parcels for delinquency/municipal lien context.

### Step 3: Governance / Management
- Ask for board roster, current management contract, management company, CAM credentials, minutes, budgets, reserve study, insurance policies, and tax returns.
- Search CT DCP for manager licensing/complaint context if manager is named.
- Search CT Judicial by HOA name and management company.

### Step 4: Winter Loss / Claim Oversight
- Intake claim file: date of loss, carrier, policy, claim number, adjuster, public adjuster, restoration contractor, engineer, roofer, remediation contractor, scope, estimate, supplement, payments, disputed items, photos, and resident communications.
- Verify public adjuster licensing if applicable.
- Build claims dashboard: status, responsible party, next action, due date, cost exposure, board decision needed.

### Step 5: Board-Facing Proposal
- Position Camelot as executive operational partner.
- Emphasize hybrid management model: executive oversight + financial layer + field operations + claims/vendor coordination + technology reporting.
- Include 30-60-90 transition plan and service structure.
